Concept & Complexity

Breadth-First Search (BFS) explores a graph layer by layer. It starts at a chosen node, visits all its immediate neighbors, and only then moves on to the neighbors' neighbors.

It uses a Queue (First-In-First-Out) to keep track of which nodes to visit next. Watch the queue update on the left as nodes are pushed and popped!
Time: O(V + E)
Space: O(V)
Where V = Vertices (Nodes) and E = Edges.
